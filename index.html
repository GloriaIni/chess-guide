THE BEAUTY OF CHESS

Brain chess is a fascinating game, with the primary objective of checkmating your opponent's king.

A standard chessboard at the beginning of a game contains 32 pieces in total: 16 pieces for each player. Each player has 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 knights, 2 bishops, and 8 pawns. So, there are 16 pieces for white and 16 pieces for black. Get to know these piece!

Hi, I am Mr Pawn

Get to know me!

  • I move forward one square at a time. However, on my initial move, I have the option to move forward two squares instead of one.
  • I capture diagonally.
  • I can't move backward. If a square directly in front of me is occupied by another piece, I can't move forward.
  • Anytime I get to my opponent's back rank, I get promoted to any other piece (typically a queen, but I can also be a knight, bishop, or rook).
  • I have seven brothers and eight step-sisters on the chessboard.

    • Hi, I am Rook

    Get to know me!

  • I can move any number of squares along a rank (horizontally) or a file (vertically), but I cannot jump over other pieces.

    • Hey! Call me Knight

    Get to know me!

    I am sure you all know I am one of the more unique pieces in chess due to my distinctive L-shaped movement pattern. You read that right!

  • I move in an "L" shape: two squares in one direction (either horizontally or vertically), and then one square perpendicular to that direction, or vice versa.
  • Unlike other pieces, I can "jump" over other pieces. Which means I can move to a square regardless of whether other pieces are blocking its path.

    • I am the Bishop

    Get to know me!

  • I can move any number of squares diagonally, but I cannot jump over other pieces.
  • I am white and yes, I have a black brother.
  • Since we both move diagonally, we are confined to squares of our individual color throughout the game.
  • You might want to be my friend, as I am especially powerful when positioned on long, open diagonals, and can control a large number of squares and exert significant influence over the board. Thank me later.
